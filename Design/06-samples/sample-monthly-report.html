Executive Summary

March 2026 marked a turning point for BetWarrior Brazil. The operation delivered its strongest month of the year, with GGR reaching R$149,819 (+120% MoM) and NGR at R$126,058 (+197% MoM). This recovery followed two consecutive negative months in January and February, signaling that the structural adjustments implemented in Q1 are beginning to yield results.

Acquisition efficiency improved materially: FTDs grew 49% MoM to 1,784, while conversion rate (FTDs / Full Registrations NoLock) climbed to 22.2% from 20.6% in February (+1.6pp). Bonus cost discipline was maintained at R$23,761, representing 15.9% of GGR - a significant improvement from February's 37.6%. The casino vertical continued its consistent positive contribution at R$109,340 GGR, while Sports recovered from near-zero to R$40,479.

  • GGR R$149,819 (+120% MoM) - best result since October 2025
  • NGR R$126,058 (+197% MoM) - three consecutive months of improvement
  • FTDs 1,784 (+49% MoM) - conversion rate FTD/FRNL at 22.2% (+1.6pp)
  • Bonus Cost/GGR at 15.9% - down from 37.6% in Feb, showing improved efficiency
  • Deposits R$1.65M (+15% MoM) - Withdrawals R$1.55M (-4% MoM)
  • GTM strategy fully revised - new acquisition and retention framework in place

Key Drivers

  • Casino GGR consistency: R$109K with 6.3% margin, stable revenue base across all months
  • Sports recovery: GGR turned positive at R$40K after 4 months of losses or near-zero results
  • Bonus cost discipline: Ratio dropped from 37.6% to 15.9% of GGR, improving unit economics
  • Funnel optimization: 42% more registrations flowing through with better conversion rates
  • Deposit growth (+15%) outpacing withdrawal growth (-4%), improving cash flow dynamics

Challenges

  • GGR at 26.4% of target (R$149K vs R$567K) - significant gap to close in coming months
  • FTDs at 29.7% of target (1,784 vs 6,000) - acquisition scale remains below plan
  • Unique Bettors at 16.7% of plan (3,105 vs 18,577) - retention and reactivation need focus
  • Sports margin at 2.0% - still below industry benchmarks of 13-15%
  • Bet volume (R$3.8M) declined 62% from November peak (R$12.0M)

Target Recalibration — Work in Progress

Context

Current targets were set before operational reality was fully understood. The significant gap between actual results and planned targets (GGR at 26% of plan, FTDs at 30% of plan) reflects assumptions that did not account for the structural challenges encountered during the first months of regulated operation.

Diagnostic Completed

A comprehensive diagnostic of structural blockers has been completed across all operational areas. The assessment covered tracking infrastructure, onboarding flows, agency capabilities, creative production, and governance models. Findings are being used to define realistic Q2 targets based on actual run rates and measurable improvement trajectories.

New Targets for Q2

Revised targets are being defined for Q2 2026 based on three inputs: (1) actual Jan-Mar run rates as baseline, (2) expected uplift from resolved blockers, and (3) phased channel ramp-up timelines. The new framework will be presented for approval in April with monthly checkpoints built in.

Key Blockers Identified
Tracking & Tagging Gaps
FTD pixel was misconfigured until March 13th, causing attribution errors across all paid channels. Media spend decisions were being made on incomplete data. Tagging fix deployed mid-month; full impact expected in April.
Onboarding KYC Friction
Document verification flow causing significant drop-off. Users abandoning after initial registration due to multi-step KYC requirements.
Creative Production Capacity
Limited in-house creative output constraining campaign velocity. Dependency on external agency with slow turnaround times.
Media Attribution Accuracy
Incomplete UTM coverage and fragmented tracking across paid, organic and affiliate channels making ROI analysis unreliable.

Revenue vs. Forecast

GGR closed at R$149.8K — 74% below the monthly forecast of R$569K. The gap remains a critical indicator that recovery volumes are not yet tracking with the aggressive growth projections.

NGR reached R$126K, missing the R$455K forecast by 72%. Although NGR is increasing month-over-month, the operation is still failing to reach the profitability levels planned for the end of Q1.

On a year-to-date basis, cumulative NGR is R$63.5K — representing a 87% variance against cumulative forecast. BW is effectively R$432K behind the net revenue plan.

Costs & Investments

Staff costs are 7.5x higher than the NGR produced in March. While the ratio improved compared to February, it still reflects a high fixed-cost structure built for a user volume that the marketing funnel has yet to deliver.

Professional services reached R$77.8K, exceeding the monthly forecast by 141%. Cumulative professional fees are now 177% over plan.

Marketing spend had a -29% variance (R$2.5M actual vs R$3.5M planned). This continued underspending can be understood as a lag driver for the missed FTD targets, directly impacting the current and future revenue potential of the operation.
